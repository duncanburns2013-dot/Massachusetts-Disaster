# SOLUTION 1: END THE SHELTER CRISIS

## Current Situation
- **Annual Cost:** $1+ Billion
- **Pre-crisis cost:** $324M/year ($27M/month)
- **Current cost:** $900M+/year ($75M/month)
- **Peak capacity:** 7,500 families
- **Average stay:** 16 months
- **Cost per family:** $10,000+/month

## The Problem
Massachusetts is the **ONLY state with a right-to-shelter law.** This has made us a magnet for:
- Migrants from the southern border
- Residents of other states seeking benefits
- Those unwilling to accept available work

## The Reforms

### 1.1 Repeal or Limit Right-to-Shelter
- Limit right-to-shelter to Massachusetts **residents** (defined as 1+ year residency)
- Require legal status verification
- End shelter access for those who refuse available housing

### 1.2 Enforce Time Limits
- **9-month maximum** strictly enforced
- Extensions only for documented job search efforts
- Automatic exit for those declining employment

### 1.3 Work Requirements
- Able-bodied adults must accept available work within 90 days
- Job training participation mandatory
- Refusal = loss of benefits

### 1.4 Federal Coordination
- Cooperate with federal immigration enforcement
- Advocate for federal reimbursement
- Pressure Congress on border security
- Decline to participate as sanctuary jurisdiction

### 1.5 Return to Sustainable Levels
- Target: 4,100 families (pre-crisis level)
- Close excess hotel contracts
- Prioritize Massachusetts residents with documented history

## Savings: $700M+ Annually

| Current | Reformed | Savings |
|---------|----------|---------|
| $1B+/year | $300M/year | **$700M+** |

# SOLUTION 2: OVERTIME ABUSE CRACKDOWN

## Current Situation
- **Total State Payroll:** $10.04 Billion
- **Total Overtime:** $599 Million (6.0%)
- **Worst Offender:** Suffolk Sheriff at 27.4%

ðŸ“Š **See: charts/overtime_abuse.svg**

## The Reforms

### 2.1 Mandatory Caps
- **10% maximum** overtime as percentage of departmental payroll
- Departments exceeding cap lose budget authority
- Waivers only from Governor with public justification

### 2.2 Hire vs. Overtime Analysis
- For every position with 50%+ overtime, evaluate new hire cost
- Mandatory hiring when new hire is cheaper than OT
- Annual report to Legislature

### 2.3 Suffolk Sheriff Special Audit
- Immediate Inspector General investigation
- 27.4% overtime suggests systemic abuse
- Personal liability for managers approving excess OT

### 2.4 Public Transparency
- All overtime published online by employee name
- Monthly reports to Legislature
- Automatic alerts when department exceeds 10%

### 2.5 Pension Impact
- Exclude overtime from pension calculations
- End "pension spiking" in final years

## Savings: $200M+ Annually

- **Annual Appropriation:** $5.1+ Billion

## The Reforms

### 3.1 Hybrid Plan for New Hires
- All employees hired after July 1, 2026: hybrid plan
- 50% defined benefit / 50% 401k-style
- Portable if employee leaves state service

### 3.2 End Pension Spiking
- Calculate benefits on career average salary
- Exclude overtime from calculations entirely
- 5-year averaging minimum

### 3.3 Retirement Age Reform
- Minimum retirement age: 62
- Full benefits at 65
- Early retirement actuarially reduced

### 3.4 Benefit Caps
- Maximum pension: $100,000 (COLA-adjusted)
- Annual COLA: Lesser of 3% or CPI
