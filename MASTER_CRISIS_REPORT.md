## PART 1: THE JOBS CRISIS

### Private Sector Employment: 3rd Worst in America

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics and Pioneer Institute analysis (May 2025), Massachusetts' private sector has **contracted 0.74%** since January 2020—one of only four states plus D.C. to experience negative growth.

**Ranking of Worst States:**
1. District of Columbia: -5.39%
2. Hawaii: -2.75%
3. Vermont: -1.00%
4. **Massachusetts: -0.74%**

**Meanwhile, Competitor States Are Booming:**
- Florida: +11.8%
- Texas: +10.9%
- North Carolina: +10.1%

### The Income Gap

- Average income of leavers: **$109,902**
- Average income of arrivers: **$100,920**

### The Millionaire Tax Debate

The 4% surtax on incomes over $1 million (passed 2022) has generated **$5.7 billion total** but:
- 90% of CPAs surveyed say high-income clients are considering leaving
- 55% say the surtax is the **primary reason** for leaving
- Tufts analyst: "For every dollar the millionaire's tax brings in, the state loses 20-50 cents from those who leave or avoid"

## PART 9: SOLUTIONS

### Total Potential Savings: $5.2-6.0 Billion/Year

| Reform Area | Annual Savings |
|-------------|----------------|
| Electricity Reform | $2.0-3.0 billion |
| Healthcare Verification | $800 million |
| Shelter Reform | $640-700 million |
| Pension Reform | $500 million (long-term) |
| Education Consolidation | $400 million |
| Procurement Reform | $300 million |
| Overtime Crackdown | $200 million |
| Workforce Rightsizing | $200 million |
| MBTA Efficiency | $150 million |

**Per Household Savings: $1,960-2,265/year**

### Specific Reforms Needed

**Electricity:**
- Cap Mass Save at $1.0B (from $1.5B)
- Consolidate overlapping solar programs
- Reform RPS multipliers
- Implement affordability caps

**Healthcare/Shelter:**
- Annual eligibility verification
- IRS/SSA matching for benefits
- Enforce 9-month shelter limits
- Work requirements where appropriate

**Pensions:**
- Hybrid plans for new employees (50% DB, 50% DC)
- End benefit spiking
- Minimum retirement age 62

**Operations:**
- Cap overtime at 10% of department payroll
- Require minimum 3 bids for contracts
- Consolidate school districts under 1,000 students
- 2% annual attrition without replacement
