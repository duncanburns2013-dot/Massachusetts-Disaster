## Part 2: The Migration Crisis - Emergency Shelter Costs

### Shelter System Spending

**Source:** State Budget Reports, Boston Herald, Boston Globe, GBH News

| Fiscal Year | Spending | Notes |
|-------------|----------|-------|
| FY2024 | $856-932 Million | Final actual |
| FY2025 (projected) | $1+ Billion | As of March 2025, already $520M spent |
| **Total Since FY24 Start** | **$1.3+ Billion** | And climbing |

### Cost Per Family

- **$10,000/month per family** (Senate Ways & Means estimate)
- **$64/day per person for food alone** ($16 breakfast, $17 lunch, $31 dinner)
- **7,500 families at peak capacity** (now reduced to ~5,600)
- **Average length of stay:** 16 months
- **Approximately 50% are migrants**

### Pre-Crisis vs. Crisis Costs

| Period | Monthly Cost | Families |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| Pre-Crisis (Normal) | $27 Million | ~4,100 |
| August 2023 | $45 Million | Growing |
| Early 2024 | $75 Million | 7,500+ |
| Late 2024 | $75-78 Million | Capped at 7,500 |

**Bottom Line:** Shelter costs have nearly TRIPLED from normal operations.

## Part 5: Healthcare Spending - MassHealth

### Total Healthcare Expenditures

**Source:** CHIA (Center for Health Information and Analysis) 2025 Annual Report

| Category | FY2023 Spending | Change from FY2022 |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Total Healthcare (THCE)** | **$78.1 Billion** | +8.6% |
| Commercial Insurance | $27.4 Billion | +7.2% |
| Medicare | $22.9 Billion | +7.1% |
| **MassHealth (Medicaid)** | **$22.2 Billion** | +14.8% |
| Net Cost Private Insurance | $3.3 Billion | +6.5% |
| VA + Health Safety Net | $2.2 Billion | -1.8% |

### MassHealth (State Medicaid) Detail

- **Per Capita Healthcare:** $11,153 per Massachusetts resident
- **MassHealth Enrollment:** ~2.3 million residents (1/3 of state population)
- **Growth Rate:** 14.8% increase in one year
- **New Supplemental Payments:** $1.5 Billion added in FY2023 for hospitals

### The Problem

Healthcare spending growth (8.6%) is more than DOUBLE the benchmark (3.6%) and outpaces:
- State GDP growth (5.8%)
- Inflation (3.7%)  
- Wage growth (3.3%)
